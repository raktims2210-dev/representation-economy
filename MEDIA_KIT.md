## 500-Word Description

The Representation Economy is a conceptual and institutional framework for understanding how artificial intelligence systems interact with enterprises, governments, institutions, infrastructure, and society through machine-legible representations of reality.

The core premise is simple:

AI systems do not operate directly on reality. They operate on representations of reality.

As AI systems increasingly participate in lending decisions, healthcare systems, cybersecurity, logistics, governance, enterprise workflows, legal interpretation, autonomous execution, and public infrastructure, the decisive challenge is no longer only model intelligence. It is representation quality, contextual integrity, governance legitimacy, and trusted execution.

The framework argues that many enterprise AI failures do not originate purely from poor reasoning models. They emerge from fragmented representations, weak institutional context, identity ambiguity, broken delegation structures, legitimacy gaps, and poor execution governance.

To study these problems systematically, the repository introduces the SENSE–CORE–DRIVER architecture.

SENSE is the legibility layer where reality becomes machine-readable through signals, entities, state representation, and continuous evolution.

CORE is the cognition layer where systems reason, optimize, plan, and generate decisions or autonomous behaviors.

DRIVER is the legitimacy layer that governs delegation, identity, verification, execution, accountability, and recourse inside institutional environments.

The repository also explores broader concepts including:

- Representation Governance
- Representation Integrity
- Representation Monopolies
- Representation Fragmentation
- Representation Attack Surfaces
- Delegated Autonomy
- Institutional Intelligence
- Machine-Legible Enterprises
- Representation Quality Engineering
- DRIVEROps
- AI Legitimacy Systems

The broader goal is to establish an open conceptual foundation for understanding institutional AI systems and the economic structures emerging around machine-mediated reality.

The project is maintained publicly through GitHub and supported by persistent scholarly records on Zenodo, Figshare, HAL Open Science, OSF, ResearchGate, and Academia.edu.
