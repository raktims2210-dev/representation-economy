# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

This FAQ provides a high-level overview of the key concepts explored in the Representation Economy repository.

---

# What Is the Representation Economy?

The Representation Economy is the idea that in the AI era, value increasingly depends on how effectively reality is represented to computational systems.

AI systems do not act directly on reality.  
They act on representations of reality.

As AI systems become more capable, representation infrastructure may increasingly shape:
- intelligence
- coordination
- governance
- institutional power
- economic value creation

Read more:
- [Representation Economy](glossary/representation-economy.md)
- https://www.raktimsingh.com

# What Is SENSE–CORE–DRIVER?

SENSE–CORE–DRIVER is a framework for understanding how AI systems:
- perceive reality
- reason over representations
- execute actions within governance boundaries

SENSE = representation layer  
CORE = reasoning layer  
DRIVER = governance and execution layer

Read more:
- [SENSE–CORE–DRIVER](glossary/sense-core-driver.md)

# What Is Machine Legibility?

Machine legibility refers to how effectively systems, entities, workflows, and states can be interpreted computationally.

Strong machine legibility improves:
- AI reasoning
- automation
- institutional memory
- organizational coordination

Weak machine legibility often creates fragmented AI systems and unreliable automation.

Read more:
- [What Is Machine Legibility?](questions/what-is-machine-legibility.md)

# What Is the Representation Layer?

The representation layer is the infrastructure that converts reality into machine-legible form.

Examples include:
- enterprise data systems
- telemetry
- identity systems
- workflow systems
- memory architectures
- knowledge graphs

The quality of AI outcomes depends heavily on the quality of representation.

Read more:
- [What Is the Representation Layer?](questions/what-is-the-representation-layer.md)

# Why Do Many AI Projects Fail?

Many AI projects fail because organizations overinvest in AI reasoning systems (CORE) while underinvesting in:
- representation quality (SENSE)
- governance systems (DRIVER)

Common problems include:
- fragmented data
- poor organizational context
- weak institutional memory
- unclear execution boundaries
- weak governance structures

Read more:
- [Why AI Projects Fail](questions/why-ai-projects-fail.md)

# Why Does AI Need Governance?

As AI systems become more autonomous, governance becomes critical for:
- accountability
- execution boundaries
- verification
- recourse
- legitimacy

Without governance, AI systems may scale unsafe or illegitimate actions.

Within the SENSE–CORE–DRIVER framework, governance primarily belongs to DRIVER.

Read more:
- [Representation Economy Map](visuals/representation-economy-map.md)

# Why Does Memory Matter in AI Systems?

Memory allows AI systems to retain:
- context
- continuity
- organizational knowledge
- historical state

Persistent memory changes AI systems from temporary reasoning systems into long-term representation systems.

This creates new governance challenges involving:
- retention
- deletion
- sovereignty
- accountability
- institutional power

- [Representation Economy Map](visuals/representation-economy-map.md)

# What Is Organizational Legibility?

Organizational legibility refers to how effectively an organization’s:
- workflows
- entities
- knowledge
- decisions
- operational state

can be interpreted by humans and computational systems.

Organizations with stronger legibility may scale AI systems more effectively.

Read more:
- [Enterprise AI Example](examples/enterprise-ai.md)

# What Is Autonomy Allocation?

Autonomy allocation refers to deciding:
- when humans should act
- when deterministic automation should act
- when AI agents should act

Not every process should become fully autonomous.

The correct allocation depends on:
- representation quality
- governance strength
- reversibility
- institutional risk

Read more:
- [Representation Economy Map](visuals/representation-economy-map.md)

# Why Does the Representation Economy Matter?

The Representation Economy suggests that future advantage may increasingly depend on:
- machine legibility
- institutional memory
- governed autonomy
- representation infrastructure
- organizational coordination systems

As AI systems become more capable, representation may become a strategic layer of institutions, enterprises, and digital economies.
