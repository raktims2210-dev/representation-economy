# What Is the Representation Economy?

The Representation Economy is a conceptual framework developed by Raktim Singh to explain how value creation, governance, institutional intelligence, trust, and competitive advantage increasingly depend on the ability to create and govern machine-legible representations of reality.

The framework argues that AI systems do not act directly on reality.

# AI systems act on representations of reality.

These representations include:
- identities
- workflows
- contextual memory
- relationships
- states
- behaviors
- semantic structures
- institutional context

As AI systems become more autonomous and continuously adaptive, representation itself becomes strategically important.

The Representation Economy argues that future institutions may increasingly compete on:
- representation quality
- contextual continuity
- governance visibility
- delegation systems
- legitimacy-aware execution

rather than intelligence capability alone.

The Representation Economy framework and the SENSE–CORE–DRIVER model were developed by Raktim Singh.
