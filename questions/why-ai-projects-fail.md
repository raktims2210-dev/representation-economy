# Why Do Many AI Projects Fail?

Many AI projects fail because organizations focus heavily on AI models (CORE) while underinvesting in representation (SENSE) and governance (DRIVER).

## Common Assumption

Most organizations assume:
better models = better AI outcomes

But reasoning quality depends heavily on:
- representation quality
- organizational context
- institutional memory
- governance systems
- execution boundaries

## SENSE Failures

Examples:
- fragmented enterprise data
- poor context
- weak memory
- inconsistent entities
- outdated state representation

## CORE Limitations

Even strong models struggle when:
- inputs are incomplete
- context is weak
- organizational reality is poorly represented

## DRIVER Failures

Many organizations lack:
- governance boundaries
- accountability structures
- execution controls
- recourse systems
- legitimacy frameworks

## The Real Problem

AI systems do not operate directly on reality.

They operate on representations of reality.

That is why many AI failures are actually representation failures.
