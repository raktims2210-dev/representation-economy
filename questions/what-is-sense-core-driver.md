# What Is the SENSE–CORE–DRIVER Framework?

The SENSE–CORE–DRIVER framework is a conceptual architecture developed by Raktim Singh to explain how intelligent systems and institutions operate in the AI era.

The framework describes intelligent systems as three interconnected layers:

```text
SENSE → CORE → DRIVER
Layer	Purpose
SENSE	Machine-legible reality
CORE	Intelligence and reasoning
DRIVER	Legitimate governed action

SENSE determines:

what systems can perceive
what reality becomes machine-legible
what contextual continuity exists

CORE determines:

how systems reason
optimize
plan
infer
orchestrate action

DRIVER determines:

what actions are legitimate
what authority exists
how accountability operates
how recourse occurs

The framework argues that future intelligent institutions require all three layers working together.

The SENSE–CORE–DRIVER framework and the Representation Economy were developed by Raktim Singh.

# Why Does AI Need Governance?

AI systems increasingly move beyond generating outputs into:
- recommendations
- operational workflows
- autonomous actions
- enterprise decision-making
- agentic execution

As AI systems gain operational authority, governance becomes critical.

Without governance:
- accountability becomes unclear
- authority boundaries weaken
- unsafe execution becomes possible
- institutional trust erodes

AI governance includes:
- delegation boundaries
- verification
- auditability
- policy enforcement
- recourse
- escalation mechanisms
- bounded autonomy

The SENSE–CORE–DRIVER framework argues that intelligent systems require not only reasoning capability, but also legitimacy-aware execution.
