# AI Acts on Representations, Not Reality

AI systems do not interact with reality directly.

They interact with representations of reality.

These representations may include:
- data
- documents
- telemetry
- identity systems
- knowledge graphs
- memory systems
- workflow states
- organizational context

The quality of AI outcomes therefore depends heavily on the quality of representation.

If reality is poorly represented:
- reasoning weakens
- automation fails
- governance breaks
- decisions become unreliable

This is why many AI problems are fundamentally representation problems.

The Representation Economy studies how representations increasingly shape:
- intelligence
- coordination
- governance
- institutional power
- economic value creation
