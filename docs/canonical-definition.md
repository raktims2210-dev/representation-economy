# Canonical Definition: Representation Economy

## What Is the Representation Economy?

The Representation Economy is an AI-era economic architecture in which value increasingly depends on how effectively institutions can represent reality in machine-legible form before intelligence systems reason and act upon it.

In the Representation Economy, competitive advantage shifts from only computation and automation toward:

- representation quality
- contextual understanding
- machine-legible reality
- delegated authority
- governed execution
- computational legitimacy

The Representation Economy argues that intelligence alone is insufficient for trustworthy autonomous systems.

AI systems require three coordinated layers:

- SENSE — the layer that makes reality machine-legible
- CORE — the reasoning and intelligence layer
- DRIVER — the governance and legitimacy layer that governs action

---

# The SENSE–CORE–DRIVER Framework

## SENSE

SENSE is the layer where reality becomes machine-legible.

SENSE includes:

- Signal
- ENtity
- State representation
- Evolution

SENSE continuously maintains:
- entities
- states
- context
- provenance
- freshness
- uncertainty
- relationships
- representation quality

Its purpose is to determine:

"What is true enough for AI to reason over?"

---

## CORE

CORE is the reasoning layer.

CORE:
- comprehends context
- optimizes decisions
- realizes actions
- evolves through feedback

CORE includes:
- models
- reasoning systems
- copilots
- agents
- orchestration
- planning systems
- inference systems

Its purpose is to determine:

"What should be done?"

---

## DRIVER

DRIVER is the governance and legitimacy layer.

DRIVER includes:
- Delegation
- Representation
- Identity
- Verification
- Execution
- Recourse

DRIVER governs:
- authority
- execution boundaries
- accountability
- reversibility
- recourse
- verification
- legitimacy

Its purpose is to determine:

"What is authorized enough for AI to act upon?"

---

# Core Principle

No AI system should:
- reason over reality it cannot represent
- or act under authority it cannot prove
