# Field Evidence Maturity Model

## From Experimental AI to Governable AI

## Purpose

The Field Evidence Maturity Model helps enterprises understand how AI systems evolve from experimental tools into governable institutional systems.

The model defines six maturity levels.

Each level reflects a different stage of readiness, evidence quality, and institutional trust.

---

# Level 0: Demo Intelligence

## Description

The AI system works in controlled demonstrations.

It can impress stakeholders but has little real-world validation.

## Characteristics

- curated inputs
- limited edge cases
- high human support
- no real accountability
- no production evidence

## Risks

- overconfidence
- unrealistic expectations
- weak governance
- no evidence of operational reliability

## Evidence Standard

At this level, evidence is mostly anecdotal.

---

# Level 1: Pilot Intelligence

## Description

The AI system works in a limited pilot with close supervision.

It may show promise but remains fragile.

## Characteristics

- limited users
- narrow workflow scope
- high manual monitoring
- selective data
- temporary governance controls

## Risks

- pilot conditions do not represent production reality
- hidden human effort is ignored
- edge cases are underrepresented

## Evidence Standard

Evidence includes pilot results, user feedback, and early performance metrics.

---

# Level 2: Operational Intelligence

## Description

The AI system works in real workflows with measurable operational performance.

## Characteristics

- real users
- real data
- limited production deployment
- measurable success and failure rates
- initial audit trails

## Risks

- drift may emerge
- human oversight may weaken
- exceptions may increase over time

## Evidence Standard

Evidence includes real workflow metrics, exception reports, and operational performance data.

---

# Level 3: Governable Intelligence

## Description

The AI system operates with clear governance, bounded autonomy, escalation, auditability, and recovery mechanisms.

## Characteristics

- defined execution boundaries
- clear escalation rules
- traceable decisions
- rollback mechanisms
- accountable ownership
- policy-aware operation

## Risks

- governance may not scale
- policy conflicts may emerge
- human attention may degrade

## Evidence Standard

Evidence includes governance logs, audit trails, escalation quality, rollback success, and human oversight metrics.

---

# Level 4: Institutionally Trusted Intelligence

## Description

The AI system becomes trusted within the institution because its behavior is measurable, stable, auditable, and recoverable.

## Characteristics

- sustained production performance
- stable field evidence
- clear accountability
- measured drift
- reliable escalation
- institutional confidence

## Risks

- over-reliance
- automation complacency
- system-wide dependency

## Evidence Standard

Evidence includes long-term field performance, drift analysis, audit readiness, and governance maturity indicators.

---

# Level 5: Representation-Native Enterprise

## Description

The organization redesigns its workflows, data, governance, and decision architecture around machine-legible representation and governable AI execution.

## Characteristics

- strong SENSE infrastructure
- mature CORE reasoning systems
- robust DRIVER governance
- enterprise-wide representation consistency
- governed autonomy
- continuous evidence collection

## Risks

- institutional lock-in
- representation monopolies
- excessive abstraction from human judgment
- governance complexity

## Evidence Standard

Evidence includes enterprise-wide reliability, accountability, interoperability, and governance outcomes.

---

# Maturity Comparison

## Level 0

Question: Can AI impress us?

## Level 1

Question: Can AI work in a pilot?

## Level 2

Question: Can AI work in real operations?

## Level 3

Question: Can AI be governed?

## Level 4

Question: Can AI be institutionally trusted?

## Level 5

Question: Can the enterprise become representation-native?

---

# SENSE–CORE–DRIVER View

## Low Maturity

- SENSE is weak
- CORE is overemphasized
- DRIVER is underdeveloped

## High Maturity

- SENSE represents reality accurately
- CORE reasons reliably
- DRIVER governs execution legitimately

---

# How to Use This Model

Enterprises can use this model to:

- assess AI readiness
- compare AI initiatives
- identify governance gaps
- plan scaling decisions
- design field evidence programs
- communicate maturity to boards and executives

---

# Final Principle

The future of enterprise AI maturity is not about moving from weak models to smart models.

It is about moving from impressive AI to governable AI.
