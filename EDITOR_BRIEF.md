# Core Narrative

The central thesis is:

> AI systems do not operate directly on reality.  
> They operate on representations of reality.

As AI becomes embedded into institutions, enterprises, governments, markets, finance, logistics, healthcare, and social systems, the primary competitive and governance challenge shifts from:
- model capability
to:
- representation quality,
- institutional legibility,
- governed execution,
- and legitimacy infrastructure.

The future AI economy will therefore depend on:
- who can represent reality,
- who can structure reality,
- who can govern machine interpretation,
- and who can create trusted execution systems.

This is the Representation Economy.

# Canonical Framework

## SENSE
The legibility layer.

### S — Signal
Detection of events, traces, behaviors, and changes.

### EN — Entity
Attaching signals to identifiable entities.

### S — State Representation
Structuring the current condition of the entity.

### E — Evolution
Tracking how the state changes over time.

SENSE transforms fragmented institutional reality into machine-legible reality.

---

## CORE
The cognition layer.

### C — Comprehend Context
Interpret signals and situations.

### O — Optimize Decisions
Generate reasoning and decision pathways.

### R — Realize Action
Translate intelligence into executable outcomes.

### E — Evolve Through Feedback
Continuously adapt and improve.

CORE represents intelligence, reasoning, planning, orchestration, and optimization.

---

## DRIVER
The governance and legitimacy layer.

### D — Delegation
Who authorized the action?

### R — Representation
What version of reality was used?

### I — Identity
Which entity is affected?

### V — Verification
How is correctness validated?

### E — Execution
How is action carried out?

### R — Recourse
What happens when the system is wrong?

DRIVER determines whether intelligence becomes governable, legitimate, reversible, and institutionally trustworthy.
