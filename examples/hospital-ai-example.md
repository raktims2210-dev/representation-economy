# Hospital AI Example

## Why Healthcare AI Requires More Than Intelligence

Healthcare AI systems are rapidly evolving across:
- diagnostics
- clinical support
- patient monitoring
- treatment recommendations
- hospital operations
- medical imaging

But healthcare AI is not merely a reasoning problem.

It is also:
- a representation problem
- a governance problem
- a legitimacy problem

The SENSE–CORE–DRIVER framework explains why.

---

# SENSE in Healthcare

Healthcare requires longitudinal representation of patients.

SENSE includes:
- patient identity
- medical history
- medications
- allergies
- lab results
- imaging
- wearable signals
- clinical notes
- temporal continuity

Without strong SENSE:
- patient context fragments
- diagnoses lose continuity
- recommendations become unsafe

Healthcare AI requires:
- contextual memory
- evolving patient state representation
- entity continuity
- machine-legible clinical context

---

# CORE in Healthcare

CORE includes:
- diagnosis assistance
- treatment optimization
- medical summarization
- workflow orchestration
- predictive care
- risk estimation

CORE answers:

> “What should clinicians do?”

But stronger reasoning alone cannot guarantee safe healthcare.

If patient representation is weak:
- recommendations become dangerous
- hallucinations increase
- treatment context collapses

---

# DRIVER in Healthcare

Healthcare requires strong legitimacy systems.

DRIVER includes:
- physician authority
- accountability
- explainability
- consent
- verification
- auditability
- escalation
- recourse

Healthcare systems require bounded autonomy.

Not all actions should be automated.

---

# Why Healthcare AI Is Difficult

Healthcare is difficult because:
- reality evolves continuously
- representation is incomplete
- context is deeply temporal
- governance requirements are strict

The framework argues that healthcare AI success depends on balancing:
- SENSE
- CORE
- DRIVER

together.
