# Autonomous Vehicle Example

## Why Self-Driving Systems Require More Than Intelligence

Autonomous vehicles are often described as an AI reasoning problem.

But self-driving systems also require:
- machine-legible reality
- contextual continuity
- legitimacy-aware execution

The SENSE–CORE–DRIVER framework explains this.

---

# SENSE in Autonomous Vehicles

Vehicles must continuously represent reality.

SENSE includes:
- cameras
- lidar
- radar
- object detection
- mapping
- localization
- trajectory tracking
- environmental understanding

Without SENSE:
- vehicles lose situational awareness
- context collapses
- reality becomes machine-illegible

---

# CORE in Autonomous Vehicles

CORE includes:
- path planning
- decision-making
- prediction
- obstacle avoidance
- adaptive routing
- navigation reasoning

CORE answers:

> “What should the vehicle do?”

But reasoning alone is insufficient.

Incorrect representation may cause:
- unsafe navigation
- hallucinated interpretation
- planning instability

---

# DRIVER in Autonomous Vehicles

Autonomous vehicles also require legitimacy systems.

DRIVER includes:
- operational boundaries
- legal constraints
- safety verification
- override mechanisms
- accountability
- recourse
- human intervention

This determines:
- when vehicles may act
- when systems must defer
- when human escalation occurs

---

# Why Autonomous Vehicles Are Governance Systems

Self-driving systems are not merely intelligent systems.

They are:
- real-time representation systems
- contextual reasoning systems
- legitimacy-aware execution systems

The framework argues that autonomy without governance creates institutional instability.
