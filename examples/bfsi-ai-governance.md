# BFSI AI Governance Example

## How the SENSE–CORE–DRIVER Framework Applies to Banking and Financial Services

Banks are rapidly adopting AI for:
- fraud detection
- credit underwriting
- customer support
- compliance monitoring
- treasury operations
- risk management
- personalized financial products

Most discussions focus heavily on:
- model accuracy
- reasoning capability
- prediction quality

But many real-world banking AI failures originate elsewhere.

They originate from:
- fragmented customer representation
- disconnected identity systems
- weak contextual continuity
- poor delegation visibility
- governance gaps
- lack of recourse

The SENSE–CORE–DRIVER framework explains why.

---

# SENSE in BFSI

SENSE determines how the bank represents reality.

This includes:
- customer identity
- transaction history
- device fingerprints
- account relationships
- behavioral patterns
- risk signals
- contextual history

Without strong SENSE:
- fraud systems miss patterns
- credit systems misclassify customers
- AML systems lose continuity
- customer journeys fragment

SENSE in banking includes:
- KYC systems
- entity resolution
- fraud telemetry
- transaction graphs
- customer 360 systems
- contextual memory
- observability systems

---

# CORE in BFSI

CORE is the reasoning layer.

This includes:
- fraud prediction
- credit scoring
- anomaly detection
- conversational AI
- risk optimization
- portfolio analysis
- treasury forecasting

CORE answers:

> “What should the bank do?”

But stronger reasoning alone is insufficient.

If customer representation is fragmented, even advanced AI models may produce:
- false fraud alerts
- incorrect risk decisions
- biased underwriting
- governance instability

---

# DRIVER in BFSI

DRIVER governs legitimacy and accountability.

This includes:
- delegated authority
- auditability
- compliance verification
- policy enforcement
- rollback mechanisms
- escalation workflows
- explainability
- recourse

Banks require DRIVER because financial systems affect:
- money
- trust
- legal accountability
- regulatory obligations

Without DRIVER:
- autonomous systems become risky
- governance visibility collapses
- accountability becomes unclear

---

# Why BFSI Is a Representation Problem

Most banking AI failures are not pure intelligence failures.

They are:
- SENSE failures
- DRIVER failures

Examples include:
- fragmented customer identity
- disconnected fraud signals
- stale contextual memory
- weak governance visibility
- unclear delegation boundaries

The Representation Economy argues that future banking advantage may increasingly depend on:
- representation infrastructure
- contextual continuity
- governance architecture
- legitimacy-aware execution
