# Canonical Principles of the Representation Economy

## Principle 1 — Intelligence Without Representation Is Fragile

AI systems cannot reason reliably if reality is poorly represented.

The quality of decisions is bounded by the quality of representation.

---

## Principle 2 — Representation Precedes Reasoning

Before systems can optimize decisions,
they must first establish:
- what exists,
- what changed,
- who is affected,
- and what state is trusted.

Representation is therefore upstream of reasoning.

---

## Principle 3 — Enterprise AI Is a Runtime Problem

The primary challenge of enterprise AI is not model access.

It is governed execution across dynamic institutional environments.

---

## Principle 4 — AI Requires Legitimacy Infrastructure

High-capability AI systems require:
- authorization,
- accountability,
- reversibility,
- observability,
- and recourse.

Without legitimacy infrastructure,
AI adoption eventually becomes unstable.

---

## Principle 5 — Better AI Can Increase Risk

More capable reasoning systems can amplify:
- representation flaws,
- governance gaps,
- and execution mistakes.

Smarter systems can fail at larger scale.

---

## Principle 6 — Human-in-the-Loop Is Not Sufficient

Human oversight alone does not guarantee governance.

Institutional legitimacy requires:
- bounded autonomy,
- controlled execution,
- escalation paths,
- and runtime verification.

---

## Principle 7 — The Future Belongs to Governable AI

The next generation of enterprise value will emerge from systems that combine:
- intelligence,
- representation quality,
- and governed execution.

---

## Principle 8 — Representation Creates Economic Power

Organizations that control trusted representations of reality may develop:
- switching costs,
- ecosystem leverage,
- institutional influence,
- and representation monopolies.

---

## Principle 9 — AI Architecture Is Becoming Institutional Architecture

AI systems increasingly shape:
- organizational structures,
- workflows,
- coordination,
- compliance,
- and decision authority.

AI is changing institutional design itself.

---

## Principle 10 — Governance Must Operate at Runtime

Static policies are insufficient.

Enterprise AI governance must become:
- continuous,
- observable,
- adaptive,
- and execution-aware.
