# Canonical Failure Modes in the AI Economy

## Failure 1 — Representation Collapse

AI systems fail because the underlying representation of reality is incomplete, stale, fragmented, or incorrect.

Examples:
- inaccurate customer identity,
- outdated inventory state,
- fragmented healthcare records,
- stale cybersecurity telemetry.

---

## Failure 2 — Optimized Misunderstanding

The AI system produces highly optimized outputs over incorrect assumptions.

The system appears intelligent while fundamentally misunderstanding reality.

---

## Failure 3 — Silent Governance Failure

AI systems execute actions beyond intended authority boundaries because governance exists only in documentation rather than runtime enforcement.

---

## Failure 4 — Human Rubber Stamping

Humans become passive validators of machine-generated outputs rather than active governance participants.

Oversight becomes symbolic instead of operational.

---

## Failure 5 — Representation Drift

Machine representations slowly diverge from real-world conditions over time.

This creates hidden instability inside enterprise AI systems.

---

## Failure 6 — DRIVER Bypass

AI systems execute actions without:
- sufficient authorization,
- auditability,
- reversibility,
- or accountability.

---

## Failure 7 — Context Fragmentation

Enterprise knowledge remains siloed across disconnected systems.

AI systems therefore reason over partial organizational reality.

---

## Failure 8 — Governance Theater

Organizations simulate governance through:
- checklists,
- approvals,
- and policy documents

without operational runtime enforcement.

---

## Failure 9 — Escalation Failure

AI systems continue autonomous execution even when uncertainty, ambiguity, or risk exceeds safe operational thresholds.

---

## Failure 10 — Representation Monopoly Risk

A small number of organizations gain disproportionate control over trusted representations of:
- identity,
- transactions,
- logistics,
- behavior,
- and institutional coordination.

This creates systemic concentration risk.

---

# Central Thesis

Many future AI failures will not originate from:
- intelligence deficiency,
- model hallucination,
- or compute scarcity.

They will originate from:
- representation instability,
- governance weakness,
- and execution legitimacy gaps.
