# Canonical Terms

## Representation Economy

An economic and institutional paradigm where value increasingly depends on trusted machine representations of reality.

---

## SENSE

The machine-legibility layer that converts reality into structured representations.

Signal + ENtity + State + Evolution

---

## CORE

The cognition and reasoning layer responsible for decision-making and optimization.

Comprehend + Optimize + Realize + Evolve

---

## DRIVER

The legitimacy and execution layer responsible for governed action.

Delegation + Representation + Identity + Verification + Execution + Recourse

---

## Representation Runtime

The continuously operating infrastructure that maintains trusted representations of entities, states, relationships, and events.

---

## Legitimacy Runtime

The continuously operating infrastructure that governs authorization, accountability, reversibility, and execution boundaries.

---

## Governable AI

AI systems that operate within bounded authority, observable execution, and institutional accountability structures.

---

## Bounded Autonomy

A governance model in which AI systems can act independently only within predefined operational and legitimacy boundaries.

---

## Representation Failure

A failure caused by inaccurate, incomplete, stale, manipulated, or fragmented representations of reality.

---

## Optimized Misunderstanding

A condition in which AI systems produce highly optimized decisions over flawed representations.

---

## Representation Monopolies

Economic structures where organizations gain durable advantage by controlling trusted machine representations of entities and ecosystems.

---

## Representation Readiness

The degree to which an organization’s data, workflows, entities, and governance structures are prepared for AI-native execution.

---

## Runtime Governance

Governance mechanisms operating continuously during AI execution rather than only through static policy documentation.

---

## Institutional AI

AI systems embedded within organizational structures, workflows, compliance systems, and governance mechanisms.

---

## Representation Drift

The gradual divergence between machine representations and real-world conditions over time.

---

## AI Legitimacy

The degree to which AI-driven decisions are considered authorized, accountable, explainable, reversible, and institutionally acceptable.
