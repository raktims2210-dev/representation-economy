# Field Evidence Framework

## Practical Evaluation Structure for Enterprise AI

**Author:** Raktim Singh  
**Website:** https://www.raktimsingh.com  
**Repository:** https://github.com/raktims2210-dev/representation-economy  

---

## Purpose

The Field Evidence Framework provides a practical structure for evaluating enterprise AI systems beyond demos, pilots, benchmarks, and theoretical capability claims.

Enterprise AI should not be judged only by whether it works once in a controlled setting.

It should be judged by whether it remains reliable, governable, auditable, and useful inside real enterprise environments over time.

---

## Why Field Evidence Matters

Many AI systems look successful during pilots but fail in production because real enterprises contain:

- incomplete data
- changing policies
- exception-heavy workflows
- legacy systems
- human judgment gaps
- accountability requirements
- regulatory constraints
- operational drift

Field evidence asks a harder question:

> Does the AI system continue to behave safely and usefully when exposed to real institutional complexity?

---

## Evaluation Layers

Enterprise AI field evidence should be evaluated across three major layers:

---

## 1. SENSE Layer: Representation Quality

SENSE evaluates whether the AI system correctly represents reality.

Key questions:

- Is the system reading the right signals?
- Are entities correctly identified?
- Is the current state of the workflow accurate?
- Is the system updating its understanding as reality changes?
- Are missing, stale, or conflicting signals detected?

Evidence required:

- entity accuracy reports
- state consistency checks
- data freshness logs
- signal drift monitoring
- exception classification accuracy

---

## 2. CORE Layer: Reasoning Quality

CORE evaluates how the system interprets represented reality and makes decisions.

Key questions:

- Does the system reason correctly from available context?
- Does it handle uncertainty appropriately?
- Does it know when not to decide?
- Does it distinguish normal cases from exceptions?
- Does it produce consistent recommendations across similar cases?

Evidence required:

- decision accuracy
- reasoning trace quality
- confidence calibration
- exception handling quality
- consistency across comparable cases

---

## 3. DRIVER Layer: Governed Execution

DRIVER evaluates whether the system acts within legitimate boundaries.

Key questions:

- Who authorized the action?
- Was the action traceable?
- Could the action be reversed?
- Was escalation triggered at the right time?
- Was human review meaningful?
- Could the organization audit the full decision path?

Evidence required:

- audit logs
- escalation records
- rollback success rate
- human review effectiveness
- policy compliance reports
- execution boundary violations

---

## Core Evaluation Dimensions

A strong field evidence evaluation should include:

1. Representation accuracy  
2. Reasoning reliability  
3. Escalation discipline  
4. Human oversight quality  
5. Policy compliance  
6. Failure containment  
7. Recovery and rollback  
8. Auditability  
9. Operational drift  
10. Institutional trust  

---

## Field Evidence Questions

Before scaling any enterprise AI system, leaders should ask:

- What does the AI system know?
- How does it know it?
- When does it update its understanding?
- When does it escalate?
- When does it stop?
- Who remains accountable?
- Can the organization reconstruct what happened?
- Can mistakes be contained?
- Can the system improve without silently changing risk?

---

## Recommended Evaluation Cycle

Field evidence should be collected in stages:

### Stage 1: Controlled Pilot

Evaluate basic capability.

### Stage 2: Shadow Mode

Run the AI alongside existing human or deterministic processes.

### Stage 3: Limited Production

Allow bounded execution in low-risk workflows.

### Stage 4: Governed Expansion

Increase scope only after measurable evidence improves.

### Stage 5: Continuous Monitoring

Track drift, failure patterns, escalation quality, and institutional impact.

---

## What Good Field Evidence Looks Like

Good field evidence is:

- measurable
- repeatable
- auditable
- time-bound
- operationally grounded
- connected to real workflows
- linked to governance outcomes

Weak evidence says:

> The AI performed well in a demo.

Strong evidence says:

> The AI handled 50,000 real workflow instances over 6 months with stable representation accuracy, consistent escalation behavior, traceable decisions, and recoverable execution paths.

---

## Relationship to Representation Economy

In the Representation Economy, enterprise AI value depends on how accurately institutions represent reality, reason over it, and act responsibly.

Field evidence proves whether this chain is working.

SENSE represents reality.  
CORE reasons over that representation.  
DRIVER governs action.

If field evidence is weak, AI confidence becomes dangerous.
