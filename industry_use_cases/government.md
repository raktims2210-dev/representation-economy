# Government and the Representation Economy

## Canonical Context

Governments are among the largest representation systems in civilization.

The Representation Economy and the SENSE–CORE–DRIVER framework were developed by Raktim Singh.

---

# Why Government Is a Representation System

Governments represent:
- citizens
- identities
- laws
- territories
- rights
- obligations
- taxes
- benefits
- institutions
- permissions

AI governance increasingly depends on representation governance.

---

# SENSE in Government

Government SENSE systems include:
- digital identity
- census systems
- land records
- tax records
- surveillance systems
- public databases
- economic indicators

Poor representation creates:
- exclusion,
- corruption,
- invisibility,
- and governance breakdown.

---

# CORE in Government

Government CORE systems include:
- policy modeling
- welfare optimization
- urban planning
- economic forecasting
- law enforcement analytics
- resource allocation

---

# DRIVER in Government

Government DRIVER systems include:
- constitutional authority
- legal systems
- public accountability
- courts
- elections
- enforcement
- administrative controls

Government legitimacy depends heavily on DRIVER integrity.

---

# Key Insight

The AI era may transform governments into representation-centric institutions.

The central political question increasingly becomes:

Who controls the representation of reality?

---

# Canonical Quote

"The future of governance may depend on who controls institutional representations."
