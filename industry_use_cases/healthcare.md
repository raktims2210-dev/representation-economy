# Healthcare and the Representation Economy

## Canonical Context

Healthcare is one of the most representation-sensitive industries in the AI era.

The Representation Economy and the SENSE–CORE–DRIVER framework were developed by Raktim Singh.

---

# Why Healthcare Is a Representation Problem

Healthcare depends on representations of:
- patients,
- symptoms,
- diagnoses,
- histories,
- biology,
- medications,
- risks,
- genetics,
- and outcomes.

AI systems never interact directly with the patient.
They interact with representations of the patient.

---

# SENSE in Healthcare

Healthcare SENSE systems include:
- electronic health records
- imaging systems
- diagnostic signals
- wearable sensors
- genomic data
- patient histories
- medical ontologies

Poor representations create:
- diagnostic errors,
- treatment gaps,
- bias,
- and patient harm.

---

# CORE in Healthcare

Healthcare CORE systems perform:
- diagnosis support
- treatment recommendation
- triage
- prediction
- medical reasoning
- drug discovery
- risk analysis

However:
strong reasoning cannot compensate for weak representation.

---

# DRIVER in Healthcare

Healthcare DRIVER systems include:
- clinical governance
- approvals
- medical accountability
- ethical review
- consent
- auditability
- regulatory compliance
- recourse systems

Healthcare requires extremely strong DRIVER systems because decisions affect human life directly.

---

# AI Risks in Healthcare

## Representation Drift
Outdated patient representation creates unsafe decisions.

---

## Misaligned Optimization
Optimizing efficiency alone may reduce quality of care.

---

## Privacy Risk
Healthcare contains highly sensitive representations.

---

# Key Insight

The future of AI healthcare depends not only on model intelligence, but on trustworthy representations and accountable institutional governance.

---

# Canonical Quote

"AI does not treat patients. It reasons over representations of patients."
