# Open Questions

## Representation Economy & SENSE–CORE–DRIVER

This document lists important open questions, unresolved problems, research directions, and conceptual tensions related to:

- Representation Economy
- SENSE–CORE–DRIVER
- Institutional AI
- Machine-legible reality
- Enterprise AI governance
- Delegated autonomy
- Representation infrastructure
- AI legitimacy systems

The purpose of this document is not to provide final answers.

Instead, it is intended to identify the major unresolved questions that may shape the future of AI systems, enterprises, institutions, governance architectures, and machine-mediated economies.

---

# Canonical Principle

> AI systems do not act directly on reality.  
> They act on representations of reality.

If this principle is correct, then many of the most important AI questions become representation questions, governance questions, and legitimacy questions.

---

# Part I — Representation Questions

---

# 1. What Makes a Representation Trustworthy?

AI systems increasingly depend on representations.

But what actually makes a representation trustworthy?

Possible dimensions include:

- correctness,
- provenance,
- freshness,
- contextual continuity,
- governance alignment,
- explainability,
- verification,
- and institutional acceptance.

Open question:

> Is representation trust fundamentally technical, institutional, or social?

---

# 2. How Much Representation Is Enough?

AI systems can continuously accumulate:

- signals,
- telemetry,
- context,
- state,
- and behavioral traces.

But more representation does not automatically create more understanding.

Open questions:

- Is there an optimal representation density?
- When does representation become noise?
- What creates representation saturation?
- How should systems forget or decay outdated context?

---

# 3. Can Institutions Maintain Representation Continuity at Scale?

Modern enterprises often contain:

- fragmented systems,
- disconnected workflows,
- duplicated entities,
- inconsistent state,
- and siloed operational memory.

Open questions:

- Can large institutions maintain coherent machine-legible reality?
- Will representation fragmentation become a permanent condition?
- Will future enterprises compete primarily through representation coherence?

---

# 4. Who Owns Representation Infrastructure?

Representation systems increasingly shape:

- access,
- trust,
- visibility,
- coordination,
- and execution authority.

Open questions:

- Who controls critical representations?
- What becomes a representation monopoly?
- Can representation infrastructure remain open and interoperable?
- Will future economic power concentrate around representation systems?

---

# 5. Can Representation Integrity Become a Strategic Discipline?

Cybersecurity became a formal discipline because systems required protection.

Open questions:

- Will representation integrity become a new institutional discipline?
- Will organizations require “representation assurance” teams?
- What metrics define representation quality?
- How should representation integrity be audited?

---

# Part II — SENSE Questions

---

# 6. What Is the Minimum SENSE Required for Reliable AI?

Many AI failures originate from weak representation layers.

Open questions:

- What minimum signal quality is required?
- How much context is enough?
- What level of entity resolution is necessary?
- How should uncertainty in SENSE be represented?

---

# 7. How Should Systems Handle Ambiguous Reality?

Reality is often:

- incomplete,
- contradictory,
- uncertain,
- evolving,
- and context-dependent.

Open questions:

- Can SENSE fully represent ambiguity?
- Should systems represent uncertainty explicitly?
- How should conflicting representations coexist?
- What happens when institutions disagree on representation?

---

# 8. What Happens When SENSE Is Manipulated?

As AI systems rely more heavily on representations, representation manipulation becomes strategically important.

Open questions:

- What are representation attacks?
- How can adversarial actors poison institutional representations?
- How should representation provenance be secured?
- Will representation manipulation become a major cybersecurity domain?

---

# Part III — CORE Questions

---

# 9. Can Better CORE Compensate for Weak SENSE?

The framework claims:

> Better CORE cannot fully fix broken SENSE.

But the degree of compensation remains unclear.

Open questions:

- How far can reasoning systems compensate for incomplete representation?
- Can AI infer missing institutional context?
- What are the limits of reasoning under representation uncertainty?
- Will future AI systems dynamically repair weak SENSE?

---

# 10. What Is the Future Relationship Between Reasoning and Representation?

Historically, AI research often emphasized reasoning.

But modern systems increasingly depend on representation infrastructure.

Open questions:

- Will representation become more important than reasoning?
- Will future AI competition shift from model scale to representation quality?
- Are we moving from intelligence-centric AI toward representation-centric AI?

---

# 11. How Should AI Systems Handle Conflicting Objectives?

CORE systems often optimize toward objectives.

But institutions contain:

- competing priorities,
- ambiguous trade-offs,
- political constraints,
- and conflicting incentives.

Open questions:

- Can optimization remain institutionally aligned?
- How should AI systems balance competing institutional goals?
- What happens when optimization conflicts with legitimacy?

---

# 12. Can AI Systems Develop Institutional Understanding?

Current AI systems reason largely through pattern recognition and contextual inference.

Open questions:

- Can AI systems truly understand institutional structures?
- What constitutes institutional intelligence?
- Can models internalize governance norms?
- Will future AI systems reason over institutional legitimacy directly?

---

# Part IV — DRIVER Questions

---

# 13. What Creates AI Legitimacy?

An AI system may be:

- intelligent,
- accurate,
- and operationally useful,

yet still lack legitimacy.

Open questions:

- What actually creates institutional trust?
- Is legitimacy technical, legal, organizational, or social?
- Can legitimacy be automated?
- Who ultimately decides legitimacy?

---

# 14. How Much Autonomy Should Be Delegated?

Delegated autonomy is becoming central to AI systems.

Open questions:

- What decisions should remain human?
- What decisions should become automated?
- What decisions require hybrid governance?
- How should autonomy levels be assigned?

---

# 15. Can Recourse Scale?

Institutional legitimacy often requires recourse.

But large-scale AI systems may generate billions of decisions.

Open questions:

- Can recourse mechanisms scale operationally?
- How should appeals work in autonomous systems?
- What becomes the institutional cost of recourse?
- Can recourse itself become automated?

---

# 16. What Happens When AI Systems Conflict With Institutional Structures?

AI systems may optimize for:

- speed,
- efficiency,
- prediction quality,
- and operational throughput.

Institutions often optimize for:

- accountability,
- fairness,
- governance,
- legitimacy,
- and stability.

Open questions:

- Will institutions adapt to AI systems?
- Or will AI systems adapt to institutions?
- What becomes the long-term equilibrium?

---

# 17. What Does Verification Mean in AI-Mediated Systems?

Traditional verification often assumes deterministic systems.

Modern AI systems are probabilistic and evolving.

Open questions:

- How should verification operate in non-deterministic systems?
- What constitutes sufficient evidence for execution?
- Can verification remain continuous rather than static?
- What becomes “provable” in AI governance systems?

---

# Part V — Institutional Questions

---

# 18. Will Future Enterprises Become Representation Systems?

Many enterprises increasingly operate through:

- digital twins,
- knowledge graphs,
- AI orchestration,
- semantic systems,
- and machine-operable workflows.

Open questions:

- Will enterprises evolve into representation-native systems?
- Will enterprise competitiveness depend on representation continuity?
- What becomes the “operating system” of institutional intelligence?

---

# 19. Will AI Create New Institutional Layers?

Historically, new technologies often create new institutions.

Open questions:

- Will AI create:
  - delegation agencies,
  - representation auditors,
  - legitimacy certification systems,
  - runtime governance infrastructure providers,
  - or representation assurance markets?

---

# 20. What Happens When Representation Becomes More Valuable Than Physical Assets?

In many digital systems, representation increasingly determines:

- visibility,
- trust,
- access,
- coordination,
- and operational capability.

Open questions:

- Can representation become a primary economic asset class?
- What happens when representation control shapes economic participation?
- How should institutions govern representation power?

---

# Part VI — Societal Questions

---

# 21. Can Human Institutions Keep Up With Machine-Mediated Reality?

AI systems may operate faster than institutional adaptation cycles.

Open questions:

- Can governance evolve quickly enough?
- Can institutions maintain legitimacy in autonomous environments?
- Will institutional trust increase or erode?

---

# 22. What Happens When Humans No Longer Fully Understand Institutional Representations?

As representation systems become increasingly complex:

- humans may no longer fully interpret them,
- yet institutions may still depend on them operationally.

Open questions:

- Can legitimacy survive representation opacity?
- How much institutional complexity can humans govern?
- What happens when representations become machine-native rather than human-native?

---

# 23. What Is the Future Relationship Between Humans and Representation Systems?

Historically, humans interpreted reality directly.

Increasingly, humans interact through machine-mediated representations.

Open questions:

- Will humans become dependent on institutional representation systems?
- Will representation systems shape perception itself?
- What happens when machine representations mediate most institutional decisions?

---

# Part VII — Framework Questions

---

# 24. Is SENSE–CORE–DRIVER Sufficient?

The framework currently contains three layers.

Open questions:

- Are additional layers required?
- Does DRIVER eventually split into multiple layers?
- Should institutional memory become a separate layer?
- How should societal coordination be represented?

---

# 25. Can the Framework Be Operationalized?

The framework is intentionally conceptual.

Open questions:

- How should organizations implement SENSE–CORE–DRIVER operationally?
- What metrics evaluate maturity?
- What architectures best support representation integrity?
- How should governance-aware execution be standardized?

---

# 26. Can Representation Economy Become a Formal Academic Discipline?

Open questions:

- Does the Representation Economy require:
  - formal economic models,
  - governance theory,
  - systems theory,
  - institutional theory,
  - and AI systems research integration?
- What would a formal academic discipline look like?
