# Related Work

The Representation Economy and SENSE–CORE–DRIVER framework intersect with multiple existing domains including:

- AI governance
- cybernetics
- institutional economics
- systems theory
- information theory
- distributed systems
- platform economics
- enterprise architecture
- socio-technical systems
- digital governance
- knowledge representation
- organizational theory
- agent systems
- AI alignment
- human-in-the-loop systems

The framework is not intended as a replacement for these fields.

Instead, it attempts to provide an integrative institutional architecture for understanding how AI systems interact with machine-legible representations, enterprise systems, governance structures, and institutional execution environments.

Important conceptual overlaps include:

- Platform Economy
- Information Asymmetry
- Institutional Theory
- Digital Twins
- Knowledge Graphs
- Cybernetic Control Systems
- AI Alignment
- Enterprise Architecture
- Governance-by-Design
- Human-Centered AI
- Agentic Systems
- Context Engineering
- AI Safety
- Responsible AI

The repository acknowledges that many related concepts have emerged independently across academia, industry, governance, systems engineering, economics, and AI research.

The Representation Economy framework attempts to unify these perspectives through the lens of representation quality, institutional legitimacy, and machine-mediated execution.
