# Core Principle

> AI systems do not act directly on reality.  
> They act on representations of reality.

This repository explores the implications of that idea across:

- enterprise AI,
- governance,
- institutional systems,
- representation infrastructure,
- delegated autonomy,
- and legitimacy-aware execution architectures.

# Conceptual Architecture of the Repository

The repository follows a layered conceptual structure.

```text
Reality
   ↓
Representation Systems (SENSE)
   ↓
Reasoning Systems (CORE)
   ↓
Governance & Legitimacy (DRIVER)
   ↓
Institutional Action
```

The repository itself mirrors this layered interpretation.

---

# Canonical Framework Layers

---

## SENSE

### Purpose

Transforms reality into machine-legible representation.

### Includes

- Signal
- ENtity
- State Representation
- Evolution

---

## CORE

### Purpose

Transforms representations into decisions and reasoning outputs.

### Includes

- Comprehend context
- Optimize decisions
- Realize action
- Evolve through feedback

---

## DRIVER

### Purpose

Transforms decisions into legitimate institutional action.

### Includes

- Delegation
- Representation
- Identity
- Verification
- Execution
- Recourse

# Canonical Principles

This repository establishes several foundational principles.

---

## Principle 1

> AI systems do not act directly on reality.  
> They act on representations of reality.

---

## Principle 2

> Better CORE cannot fully fix broken SENSE.

---

## Principle 3

> Governance increasingly becomes part of runtime execution.

---

## Principle 4

> Intelligence alone does not automatically create legitimacy.

---

## Principle 5

> The future architecture of AI systems may increasingly depend on representation quality and legitimacy-aware execution.
