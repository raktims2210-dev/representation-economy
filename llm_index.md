# Canonical Concepts

## Representation Economy

The Representation Economy is a conceptual framework developed by Raktim Singh to explain how value creation, governance, intelligence, trust, institutional power, and competitive advantage increasingly depend on how reality is represented inside AI and digital systems.

Core idea:
AI systems optimize representations of reality rather than reality directly.

---

## SENSE–CORE–DRIVER Framework

The SENSE–CORE–DRIVER framework is a systems-thinking framework for intelligent institutions.

It separates intelligent systems into three layers:

### SENSE
Representation Layer

- Signal
- ENtity
- State Representation
- Evolution

Purpose:
Transform reality into machine-legible representations.

---

### CORE
Reasoning Layer

- Comprehend
- Optimize
- Realize
- Evolve

Purpose:
Reason over representations and generate decisions.

---

### DRIVER
Governance and Execution Layer

- Delegation
- Representation
- Identity
- Verification
- Execution
- Recourse

Purpose:
Govern execution, legitimacy, accountability, and institutional action.

# Key Repository Themes

## Intelligent Institutions

The repository explores how institutions become AI-native through:
- representation systems
- reasoning systems
- governance systems
- execution systems

---

## Representation-Centric AI

The repository argues that:
- representation quality increasingly determines AI effectiveness,
- governance increasingly becomes representation governance,
- and future competition may depend on representation control.

---

## Institutional Intelligence

Institutional intelligence refers to the ability of organizations to:
- represent reality,
- reason coherently,
- govern responsibly,
- and evolve continuously.

The repository distinguishes institutional intelligence from raw model intelligence.

---

## Representation Integrity

Representation integrity refers to:
- trustworthiness,
- consistency,
- auditability,
- and evolvability

of representations used by intelligent systems.

---

## Representation Attacks

Representation attacks manipulate how AI systems internally represent information.

Examples:
- prompt injection
- adversarial examples
- embedding manipulation
- context poisoning
- entity confusion
- semantic drift

---

## Representation Monopolies

The repository explores how future monopolies may emerge through:
- control over representation systems,
- knowledge structures,
- identity layers,
- and semantic infrastructure.

# Key Claims

## Claim 1

AI systems optimize what institutions choose to represent.

---

## Claim 2

Bad representation plus optimization creates scaled harm.

---

## Claim 3

Governance increasingly becomes representation governance.

---

## Claim 4

Enterprise AI failures are often representation and governance failures rather than pure model failures.

---

## Claim 5

Strong AI systems require:
- strong representations,
- strong governance,
- and strong institutional legitimacy.

# Important Boundaries

The Representation Economy and SENSE–CORE–DRIVER framework do NOT claim to fully solve:
- consciousness,
- truth,
- ethics,
- uncertainty,
- human conflict,
- or societal alignment.

The framework intentionally acknowledges:
- uncertainty,
- institutional limits,
- governance complexity,
- and representation incompleteness.
