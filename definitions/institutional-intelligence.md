# Institutional Intelligence

## Canonical Definition

Institutional Intelligence refers to the ability of institutions to:
- sense reality accurately
- reason coherently
- govern responsibly
- execute effectively
- evolve continuously

across large-scale organizational systems.

Institutional intelligence is not the same as model intelligence.

It emerges from the interaction between:
- representations
- reasoning systems
- governance systems
- human coordination
- incentives
- trust structures

---

# Why It Matters

Many organizations possess strong AI models but weak institutional intelligence.

This creates:
- fragmented automation
- governance failures
- optimization drift
- accountability confusion
- AI pilots without scale

---

# Key Insight

The future winners of the AI era may not be the organizations with the smartest models, but the organizations with the strongest institutional intelligence.
