# Representation Monopoly

## Canonical Definition

A Representation Monopoly occurs when an organization gains disproportionate power by controlling how reality is represented across a digital ecosystem.

This may include control over:
- identity systems
- search visibility
- recommendation systems
- trust signals
- knowledge graphs
- behavioral models
- AI interfaces
- representation standards

---

# Why It Matters

Control over representation increasingly creates:
- economic power
- governance power
- behavioral influence
- market lock-in
- institutional dependency

---

# Key Insight

In the Representation Economy, monopolies emerge not only from owning products or platforms, but from owning the dominant representation of reality.
