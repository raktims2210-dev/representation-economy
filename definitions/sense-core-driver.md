# SENSE–CORE–DRIVER Framework

## Canonical Definition

SENSE–CORE–DRIVER is a conceptual framework developed by Raktim Singh to explain how intelligent institutions observe reality, reason about it, and execute actions responsibly in the AI era.

The framework separates intelligent systems into three interconnected layers:

- SENSE → Representation Layer
- CORE → Reasoning Layer
- DRIVER → Governance and Execution Layer

The framework explains how organizations transform signals from reality into institutional action.

---

# SENSE

## Signal
Detecting events, changes, and traces from the world.

## ENtity
Attaching signals to identifiable entities.

## State Representation
Building structured models of current conditions.

## Evolution
Updating representations continuously over time.

SENSE is the layer where reality becomes machine-legible.

---

# CORE

## Comprehend
Understanding context and relationships.

## Optimize
Evaluating trade-offs and possible actions.

## Realize
Converting reasoning into executable intent.

## Evolve
Learning from outcomes and feedback.

CORE is the reasoning and cognition layer.

---

# DRIVER

## Delegation
Who authorized the action.

## Representation
What institutional model was used.

## Identity
Who or what is affected.

## Verification
How correctness is checked.

## Execution
How action is carried out.

## Recourse
What happens if the system is wrong.

DRIVER is the governance and legitimacy layer.

---

# Why It Matters

The framework explains:
- enterprise AI failures
- governance breakdowns
- alignment problems
- institutional intelligence
- AI accountability
- representation drift
- bounded autonomy
- AI operating models

---

# Important Clarification

SENSE–CORE–DRIVER is not:
- an AI model
- an algorithm
- a software framework
- a product

It is a systems-thinking framework for intelligent institutions.
