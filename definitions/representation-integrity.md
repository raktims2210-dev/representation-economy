# Representation Integrity

## Canonical Definition

Representation Integrity refers to the trustworthiness, consistency, accuracy, governability, and evolvability of representations used by intelligent systems.

It ensures that representations:
- correspond sufficiently to reality
- evolve responsibly
- remain auditable
- resist manipulation
- preserve institutional trust

---

# Why It Matters

As AI systems become more autonomous, representation integrity becomes critical for:
- trust
- governance
- safety
- accountability
- alignment
- institutional resilience

Without representation integrity:
- optimization amplifies distortion
- governance weakens
- trust collapses
- decisions drift away from reality

---

# Key Insight

The AI era increasingly becomes a struggle over representation integrity.
