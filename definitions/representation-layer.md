# Representation Layer

## Canonical Definition

The Representation Layer is the layer where reality becomes machine-legible.

It transforms raw signals into structured entities, states, relationships, and contextual abstractions that AI systems can reason about.

The Representation Layer is foundational to:
- intelligent systems
- enterprise AI
- digital twins
- knowledge graphs
- embeddings
- memory systems
- AI governance

---

# Why It Matters

AI systems do not interact with reality directly.

They interact with representations of reality.

Therefore:
- representation quality determines reasoning quality
- representation integrity determines trustworthiness
- representation evolution determines adaptability

---

# Key Functions

- Entity resolution
- State tracking
- Signal interpretation
- Context modeling
- Relationship mapping
- Evolution management
