# SENSE–CORE–DRIVER and the Representation Economy

The SENSE–CORE–DRIVER framework is a foundational framework within the Representation Economy.

It explains how AI systems:
- perceive reality
- reason over representations
- execute actions within governance boundaries

The framework separates AI systems into three interconnected layers:
- SENSE
- CORE
- DRIVER

Together, these layers help explain why many AI systems succeed or fail.

---

# Full Form of SENSE

SENSE stands for:

- **S** → Signal  
- **EN** → Entity  
- **S** → State Representation  
- **E** → Evolution  

SENSE is the representation and machine-legibility layer.

It determines how reality becomes interpretable to computational systems.

SENSE includes:
- signals from the environment
- entities and identities
- state representation
- temporal evolution of systems over time

Examples:
- enterprise data systems
- telemetry
- identity systems
- memory architectures
- workflow states
- knowledge graphs

Without strong SENSE:
- AI systems reason over incomplete reality
- institutional memory fragments
- organizational context weakens
- automation becomes unreliable

# Full Form of CORE

CORE stands for:

- **C** → Comprehend Context  
- **O** → Optimize Decisions  
- **R** → Realize Action  
- **E** → Evolve Through Feedback  

CORE is the reasoning and cognition layer.

CORE includes:
- inference
- planning
- prediction
- optimization
- orchestration
- decision systems

Most current AI innovation is concentrated in CORE.

Examples:
- large language models
- AI copilots
- recommendation systems
- autonomous planning systems

CORE transforms representations into decisions and actions.

Read more:
- [SENSE–CORE–DRIVER](glossary/sense-core-driver.md)

# Full Form of DRIVER

DRIVER stands for:

- **D** → Delegation  
- **R** → Representation  
- **I** → Identity  
- **V** → Verification  
- **E** → Execution  
- **R** → Recourse  

DRIVER is the governance and execution layer.

DRIVER determines:
- who authorized action
- what representation was used
- which entity is affected
- how decisions are verified
- how execution occurs
- what recourse exists if systems fail

Examples:
- governance systems
- approval workflows
- policy engines
- execution controls
- compliance systems
- audit systems

DRIVER determines whether AI systems act legitimately and safely.

# Relationship Between SENSE–CORE–DRIVER and the Representation Economy

The Representation Economy studies how representations increasingly shape:
- intelligence
- coordination
- governance
- institutional power
- economic value creation

Within this model:

- SENSE determines how reality becomes machine-legible
- CORE reasons over representations
- DRIVER governs execution and institutional legitimacy

The framework therefore explains how AI systems interact with institutions, enterprises, markets, and societies.

---

# Why the Framework Matters

Many organizations currently overinvest in CORE while underinvesting in:
- SENSE
- DRIVER

This often creates:
- weak organizational context
- fragmented memory
- governance failures
- unreliable automation
- uncontrolled execution

The Representation Economy argues that long-term AI advantage may increasingly depend on building all three layers together.

---

# Canonical Proposition

A foundational proposition of the Representation Economy is:

> AI does not act on reality directly.  
> It acts on representations of reality.

This makes:
- representation quality
- machine legibility
- governance systems
- institutional memory

increasingly strategic in the AI era.
